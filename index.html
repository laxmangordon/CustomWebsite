Meet The Computers!

Commodore 64 (C64)

Bill Bertram - Self-published work by Bill Bertram, CC BY-SA 2.5, https://commons.wikimedia.org/w/index.php?curid=180885

Short Summary

The Commodore 64 was one of the most popular home computers in the 1980s. After being introduced on January 10th, 1982, it almost immediatly started to "fly off the shelves", selling between 12.5 and 17 million units within its lifespan.

Specifications
  • Price: $595 USD ($1,800 USD in 2022)
  • 8-bit MOS Technology 6510 CPU Processor (0.985MHz PAL / 1.023MHz NTSC)
  • 64kb dynamic RAM


VIC 20

By Evan-Amos - Own work, Public Domain, https://commons.wikimedia.org/w/index.php?curid=38582541

Short Summary

The VIC 20 was the Predecessor of the fabled C64. Released 2 years prior, it was a complete hit as well. The VIC 20 was the first computer in the world to sell over 1 million units!

Specifications
  • Price: $299.95 USD ($970 USD in 2022)
  • 8-bit MOS Technology 6502 CPU Processor (~1.108404MHz)
  • 5kb static RAM


Apple II

By FozzTexx - Own work, CC BY-SA 4.0, https://commons.wikimedia.org/w/index.php?curid=79580939

Short Summary

Realeased in June, 1977, and mainly developed by Steve Wozniak, the Apple II computer is idolized as one of the first successful mass produced personal microcomputers.

Specifications
  • Price: $1,298 USD ($6,270 USD in 2022)
  • 8-bit MOS Technology 6502 CPU Processor (~1.02272714MHz)
  • Option of 4, 8, 12, 16, 20, 24, 32, 36, 48 KiB dynamic RAM
